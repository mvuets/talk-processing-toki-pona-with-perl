The beginning (literally "first time")

In the beginning ("first time"), all the land had nothing. Much darkness was around. But God was there and was ready ("willing" or "wanted") to create. He said, "Light, be!" In no time light was shining everywhere. God called the light, "day." He also called the dark, "night."

The second day came. God said, "Sky ("great upper thing") be!" The great space in the sky came. God called it, "sky" (is there a better way to translate this?)

On the third day, God created many great waters. He also made mountains ("high land"), deserts ("flat/low land"), islands ("land in the great water"), and beaches ("land at the water's side"). He created many beautiful plants.

On the fourth day, God created the sun, moon, and stars.

On the fifth day, God created many many fish. He also created many many birds.

On the sixth day, God created many animals. He created beautiful animals and ugly animals. He created strong animals and weak animals. On this day, God also created man. God was finished creating. He said, "Everything is good."

On the seventh day, God rested.

Notes:
-I used specific numbers because I think it is one of the key points of the story and needed to be expressed. If you think otherwise, please e-mail me.
-A few of the words in this story translated pretty badly. If you see any mistakes or ways to word it better, please don't hesitate to contact me. I was very tired when I did half of this translation and may have overlooked little mistakes.
-In case you haven't noticed, the translation was aiming more for an equivalent meaning than word-for-word interpretation. Many of the words would have been too clunky to express and so something expressing the same point was substituted.
-This was translated using only the one English version children's Bible, so it can be much more accurately called a paraphrase than a translation of the Bible. I don't know enough about Hebrew to use the original version. (Thanks to Jim Henry for this note)
